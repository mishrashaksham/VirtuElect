The DMK manifesto focuses on state autonomy, social justice, and the "Dravidian Model" of inclusive growth.
A major promise is to fight for the transfer of education and healthcare back to the State List from the Concurrent List.
The party strongly opposes the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020, promising to formulate a distinct State Education Policy that emphasizes Tamil language and culture.
DMK vows to abolish the NEET medical entrance exam, arguing it disproportionately disadvantages rural and marginalized students.
The manifesto promises to secure exemptions for Tamil Nadu from any nationwide implementation of the Uniform Civil Code (UCC).
It pledges to increase reservation quotas for marginalized communities in proportion to their population, pushing beyond the current 50% cap.
For the economy, DMK promises significant investments in IT, manufacturing, and renewable energy to create jobs and maintain Tamil Nadu's industrial leadership.
The party also promises to waive educational loans for students and offer monthly financial assistance to female heads of eligible families.
The manifesto emphasizes the protection of the Tamil language, demanding it be made a co-official language of the Union Government.
