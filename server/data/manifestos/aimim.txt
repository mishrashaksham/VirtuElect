The AIMIM manifesto centers on the protection of minority rights, social justice, and equitable development for marginalized communities.
A key promise is to fight for proportionate representation of Muslims, Dalits, and OBCs in government jobs, education, and political institutions.
The party strongly opposes the implementation of a Uniform Civil Code (UCC), arguing it infringes on religious freedoms guaranteed by the Constitution.
AIMIM promises to push for a dedicated sub-plan in the national budget for the socio-economic development of minorities, similar to the SC/ST sub-plan.
It vows to repeal the Citizenship Amendment Act (CAA) and oppose the nationwide National Register of Citizens (NRC) and National Population Register (NPR).
For youth, the party promises to establish new universities and skill development centers in minority-concentrated districts.
The manifesto calls for stricter implementation of anti-discrimination laws and the establishment of an Equal Opportunity Commission.
AIMIM also promises to address the issue of wrongful incarceration of minority youth by pushing for speedy trials and compensation for the acquitted.
On the economy, the party advocates for support to weavers, artisans, and small-scale industries that employ a large number of marginalized workers.
