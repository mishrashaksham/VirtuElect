The AITC (Trinamool Congress) manifesto focuses heavily on federalism, welfare schemes, and resisting the centralization of power.
A core promise is to extend the successful "Didi Ke Bolo" and "Duare Sarkar" (government at your doorstep) models nationwide if part of the central government.
The party promises to increase the allocation for the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA) and ensure timely payment of wages.
For women, AITC promises to scale up the Lakshmir Bhandar scheme, providing monthly financial assistance to female heads of households across the country.
The manifesto strongly opposes the implementation of the Citizenship Amendment Act (CAA) and the National Register of Citizens (NRC).
It promises to double the income of farmers through better Minimum Support Prices (MSP) and comprehensive crop insurance.
The party vows to protect the linguistic and cultural diversity of India and opposes the imposition of Hindi as the sole national language.
On healthcare, AITC promises to replicate the Swasthya Sathi scheme, offering universal health coverage without capping treatment costs.
The manifesto also focuses on MSME development, promising easier access to credit and simplified GST compliance for small businesses.
