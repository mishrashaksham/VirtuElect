The BJP manifesto promises to make India the world's third-largest economy. 
It focuses on "Gyan" (Poor, Youth, Annadata/Farmers, and Nari/Women).
Key promises include expanding the Ayushman Bharat health insurance scheme to cover all senior citizens above 70 years of age.
Infrastructure development is a major priority, with plans to expand the bullet train network and modernize railway stations.
In agriculture, the party promises to continue the PM Kisan Samman Nidhi scheme and enhance crop insurance.
For youth, the focus is on creating jobs through manufacturing (Make in India) and supporting startups.
The manifesto also reiterates the commitment to implementing the Uniform Civil Code (UCC) across the country.
National security remains a key pillar, with promises to strengthen border infrastructure and defense manufacturing.
For women, the party plans to expand the Lakhpati Didi scheme to empower self-help groups.
Digital public infrastructure will be further expanded, including 5G rollout and 6G development.
