The INC manifesto focuses on "Nyay" (Justice) across five pillars: Youth, Women, Farmers, Workers, and Equity.
A central promise is a legal guarantee for Minimum Support Price (MSP) for crops, as recommended by the Swaminathan Commission.
For youth, the party promises the "Pehli Naukri Pakki" scheme, offering a guaranteed one-year apprenticeship to diploma holders and graduates.
The Mahalaxmi scheme promises to transfer ₹1 lakh per year to the oldest woman in every poor Indian family.
The manifesto proposes a nationwide socio-economic and caste census to ensure equitable distribution of resources.
It promises to increase the national minimum wage to ₹400 per day under MGNREGA.
The party vows to fill 30 lakh vacancies in the central government and abolish the Agnipath scheme for armed forces recruitment.
On healthcare, the INC promises a universal healthcare model with free medicines, diagnostics, and treatment in public hospitals.
The manifesto also pledges to review and amend laws that allegedly restrict freedom of speech and media independence.
For workers, it promises to introduce an Urban Employment Guarantee Act, similar to MGNREGA.
