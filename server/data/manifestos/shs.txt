The Shiv Sena (UBT) manifesto focuses on the rights of the 'Marathi Manoos', agricultural reform, and safeguarding the state's industrial dominance.
A central promise is to fight against the alleged shifting of major infrastructure projects and investments out of Maharashtra to other states.
The party pledges a complete waiver of farm loans and guarantees minimum support prices (MSP) for all agricultural produce.
For urban areas, particularly Mumbai, it promises significant upgrades to local train infrastructure and faster completion of the metro network.
The manifesto vows to prioritize local youth for jobs in industries operating within Maharashtra.
It promises to improve the healthcare system by upgrading rural health centers and establishing super-specialty hospitals in every district.
The party advocates for the preservation of environmental hotspots, particularly the Western Ghats, balancing development with ecological conservation.
For women, Shiv Sena (UBT) promises to strengthen self-help groups and provide interest-free loans to women entrepreneurs.
The manifesto also reiterates the party's commitment to Hindutva, but emphasizes a version that is inclusive and focuses on regional pride.
