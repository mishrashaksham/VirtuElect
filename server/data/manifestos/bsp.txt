The BSP manifesto focuses on the empowerment of Bahujans (Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Other Backward Classes, and religious minorities).
A core promise is to ensure strict implementation of reservation policies in all sectors, including the private sector and higher judiciary.
The party vows to provide "land to the landless" by redistributing surplus land to marginalized communities.
BSP strongly advocates for "Sarvajan Hitay, Sarvajan Sukhay" (welfare of all, happiness of all), promising inclusive development across all castes and religions.
The manifesto promises to focus on law and order, establishing an administration free of riots and caste-based violence, citing its past governance in Uttar Pradesh.
It opposes privatization of public sector undertakings (PSUs), arguing that it reduces job opportunities for reserved categories.
For farmers, BSP promises to ensure fair pricing for crops and timely payment of dues, particularly for sugarcane farmers.
The party pledges to provide free and high-quality education to children from marginalized communities up to the university level.
The manifesto emphasizes creating permanent employment opportunities rather than relying on temporary welfare handouts or unemployment allowances.
