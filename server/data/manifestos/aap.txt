The AAP manifesto focuses heavily on the "Delhi Model" of governance, emphasizing free utilities and robust social welfare.
A core promise is to provide free electricity (up to a certain limit) and free water supply to all households.
The party guarantees world-class, free education in government schools and free healthcare through Mohalla Clinics and public hospitals.
For women, AAP promises a monthly allowance (Samman Rashi) to empower them financially.
The manifesto strongly advocates for full statehood for Delhi, arguing it is essential for effective governance.
On employment, the party promises to end the contractualization of jobs and create new opportunities in the public and private sectors.
Anti-corruption measures remain a priority, with promises to strengthen the Jan Lokpal and establish transparent governance systems.
The manifesto includes promises to improve public transport infrastructure, including expanding the metro network and deploying electric buses.
For farmers, AAP promises to implement the Swaminathan Commission's recommendations on crop pricing.
The party also focuses on environmental issues, pledging to combat air pollution through comprehensive action plans.
